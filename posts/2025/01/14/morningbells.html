Morning Bells

1/14/25

I believe I wrote this in Fall '17. It's a curtal sonnet, which is a form pioneered by G.M. Hopkins, whom it consciously imitates in other ways.

Maybe it's the best thing I ever wrote. I don't know.

Once I was walking, waiting for morning bells
  Ringing, swinging from church-peak, blooming sound
    Weak in the distance, from bell-tower bounding down,

Every mile wider and quieter. Cast-metal spells
  And cantations of virgins, in melodies made or found
    Lying around a religion’s ruins, drown

On rose-mellow clouds, sound off curtains drawn
  To ward away morning, and the tolling knells resound
    From the shrouds, part the heavy window-gowns

And stop. The sound was gone too long, and dawn
    Rung silent through town.
