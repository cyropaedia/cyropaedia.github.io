Beignets

1/16/25

Beignets are a French donut. The French recipe doesn't use yeast, but in Louisiana I've never seen an unleavened beignet. I learned the recipe this way:

Ingredients

  • 3 cups flour, all-purpose preferred
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted

Method

Combine flour, salt, and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Combine milk, 1 tablespoon sugar, and yeast. Let the yeast get foamy. Takes maybe 4-5 minutes. Beat the egg into the wet mix, add the wet to the dry. Mix by hand. Dough will be ugly. Add the butter. Knead until dough isn't ugly. Takes me about five minutes. It will never not be sticky. Put dough ball into covered bowl to rise for an hour or two.

To cook it, roll it flat and cut it into squares. Fry in a neutral oil, coat with powdered sugar while hot. Eat them hot. Cold beignets aren't that great.
